About Us

Rao Bahadur "Dharmapravartha" Gubbi Thotadappa (1838-1910)(Gubbi), was an Indian businessman and philanthropist. He founded a free lodging place for tourists from across the nation called "Thotadappa Chathra". He was honoured with the title "Dharmapravartha" by the Maharaja of Mysore, Krishnaraja Wodeyar IV and "Rao Bahadur" by the Britishgovernment. Thotadappa was born in 1838 in a Lingayat family of Gubbi. His family moved to Bangalore in the later years where he started his business in Mamulpet.

Having no children of his own, Thotadappa decided to use all his property to the benefit of tourists and students. He founded a trust called Rao Bahadur Dharmapravartha Gubbi Thotadappa Charities (RBDGTC). In 1897, the trust bought a piece of land near Bangalore City Railway Station and, on 11 Feb 1903, Krishnaraja Wodeyar IV officially opened the Dharmachathra (for visiting tourists) and Free Hostel (for students). During his last days he donated all his property to RBDGTC trust and appointed K. P. Puttanna Chetty as first president of that trust. The trust continues its work today. This hostel facility was extended to all over Karnataka. In the year 2005, the hostel was reconstructed. For its centenary the trust built Bell Hotel at Kempegowda Bus Station as a source of income. The lodging facility offers accommodation at a nominal rate and is open to all, irrespective of their religion.Use of the hostel, however, is exclusive to students belonging to the Veerashaiva community. To date the hostel has not received Government grants. The trust awards scholarships for merit to Lingayat students every year. The Road in front of Bangalore City Railway Station was named "Gubbi Thotadappa road" in his honour.

To commemorate its valuable services to the society at largy, the is celebrating its century at by constructing and dedicating a "CENTENARY BUILDING ". The building has an excellent Auditorium at ground floor which can accommodate 800 people. it has 12860.sq ft. each of the 1st , 2nd and 3rd floor will suited for commercial purpose. The Trust has also plans to more free hostel in mofussil areas for both Girls and boys. It has also decided to grant every year ten "R.B.D.G.T.C CENTENARY SCHOLARSHIPS " of Rs 25,000/- each for poor Veerashaiva student for pursuing higher education at post gradation and super specialty level at all India level Institution any where in India.

Present Status: At Present the trust runs two free boarding and lodging hostel at bangalore one for boys and another for girls student at graduate and post-graduation leave in various discipline. lately, the trust has started in mofussil area hostel with free boarding and lodging facilities at Talkad, Kollegal and K R Nagar in mysoure district. Overall the charity is now providing free boarding and lodging facilities for more then 510 boys and Girls. In addition, every year the choultry provides accommodation for more than 2500 travellers who visit Bangalore from all over India.
